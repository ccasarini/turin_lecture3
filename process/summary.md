# Summary: 2026 State of the Nation Address (SONA)
**President Cyril Ramaphosa**
*Date: February 12, 2026*

The 2026 SONA outlines the strategic priorities of the Government of National Unity (GNU), focusing on inclusive growth, poverty reduction, and building a capable state.

## 1. Security & Organized Crime
*   **Deployment of SANDF:** Immediate deployment to Western Cape and Gauteng to combat gang violence and illegal mining.
*   **Police Recruitment:** 5,500 additional police officers to be recruited this year.
*   **Anti-Corruption:** Introduction of the **Whistle-Blower Protection Bill** and new **Public Procurement Act regulations** by mid-2026.
*   **Reform:** Establishment of a National Illicit Economy Disruption Programme and a hard-hitting criminal justice reform initiative.
*   **Vetting:** Mandatory re-vetting and lifestyle audits for senior SAPS and Metro Police management.

## 2. Infrastructure & Water Security
*   **Water Crisis:** Establishment of a **National Water Crisis Committee** chaired by the President; R156 billion committed over three years.
*   **Municipal Reform:** R54 billion incentive for metros to reform water and electricity services; revised **White Paper on Local Government** coming soon.
*   **Public Investment:** R1 trillion committed over three years for general infrastructure.
*   **Specialized Courts:** Creation of commercial courts to fast-track infrastructure-related disputes.

## 3. Energy & Logistics
*   **Renewables:** Target of 40% energy supply from renewable sources by 2030.
*   **Eskom Restructuring:** Establishing an independent transmission entity; task team to report in 3 months.
*   **Load Reduction:** Goal to eradicate load reduction by next year.
*   **Ports & Rail:** Major public-private partnerships (PPP) for port terminals and rail corridors; preparing RFP for high-speed rail.

## 4. Economic Growth & Employment
*   **Investment Target:** New target of R2 trillion in investments over the next five years.
*   **SME Support:** R2.5 billion in funding for 180,000 SMEs this year.
*   **Green Economy:** 150% tax deduction for New Energy Vehicle (NEV) investments from March 2026.
*   **Employment Stimulus:** Expansion of EPWP, CWP, and Presidential Employment Stimulus; regulatory changes for the YES programme.
*   **Disability Equity:** 7% employment equity target for persons with disabilities by 2030.

## 5. Health, Education & Social Support
*   **Child Welfare:** Mission to **end child stunting by 2030**; targeted nutrition for pregnant women.
*   **Social Grants:** Redesign of the SRD Grant this year to support livelihoods and skills.
*   **Health:** Massive rollout of **Lenacapavir** (HIV prevention) and HPV vaccines for girls 9-15; investment in academic hospitals.
*   **Education:** Making Grade R compulsory; restoring skills development levy return to 40%.
*   **Restitution:** R500 million for Phase 4 construction in District Six.

## 6. Digital Transformation & Borders
*   **Digital ID:** Launch of Digital ID this year; digitization of government services (driver's licenses, etc.) via MyMzansi platform.
*   **Borders:** Prioritized funding for border security; hiring 10,000 additional labour inspectors to enforce immigration and labor laws.

## 7. National Dialogue
*   **Public Engagement:** A nationwide National Dialogue process culminating in a **National Convention** to guide the next phase of the National Development Plan beyond 2030.
